**Justification:**

Comparing the INCOME Classic, Deluxe, and Preferred tiers: All three tiers cover the customer's core requirements for Medical Coverage, Lost/Damaged Luggage, Travel Delay, Trip Cancellation, and Hiking. However, they differ significantly in their coverage limits.

*   **Classic:** Provides the most basic limits (e.g., $250k Medical Overseas, $5k Trip Cancellation, $3k Luggage).
*   **Deluxe:** Offers a substantial increase over Classic (e.g., $500k Medical Overseas, $10k Trip Cancellation, $5k Luggage).
*   **Preferred:** Provides the highest level of coverage (e.g., $1M Medical Overseas, $15k Trip Cancellation, $8k Luggage, Unlimited Emergency Medical Evacuation for under 70s).

The customer emphasized requiring "solid Medical Coverage" for their trip to Japan, a destination known for potentially high medical costs, and indicated budget flexibility with a focus on getting the "right cover". While Deluxe offers good coverage, the Preferred tier's significantly higher limits, particularly the $1,000,000 for overseas medical expenses and unlimited emergency evacuation (for travelers under 70), best align with the customer's stated priority for robust protection.

Regarding the specific query about trip cancellation due to a sister's wedding: Standard travel insurance policies, including all INCOME tiers, typically cover cancellation only for specific unforeseen events like serious illness, accidents, or death of a close relative. Cancellation due to a change in plans for a family event like a wedding is generally *not* a covered reason under the standard "Cancelling your trip" benefit in any of the tiers. This limitation applies across Classic, Deluxe, and Preferred.

Therefore, considering the No Mix-and-Match rule and the customer's emphasis on comprehensive coverage (especially medical) and budget flexibility, the **Preferred** tier is recommended as it offers the highest limits and most robust protection for the key requirements, despite the common limitation regarding the specific wedding cancellation scenario.
