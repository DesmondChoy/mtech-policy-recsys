**Recommended Tier:** : Preferred

**Justification:**

Comparing the INCOME Classic, Deluxe, and Preferred tiers: All three standard tiers provide core coverage for medical expenses (excluding pre-existing conditions), travel delay, baggage, adventure activities (including white water rafting), and trip cancellation for *specified reasons*. However, they differ significantly in coverage limits.

*   **Medical:** Preferred offers the highest overseas medical limit ($1,000,000 for under 70s) and Unlimited evacuation/repatriation, compared to Deluxe ($500,000 medical, Unlimited evac/repat) and Classic ($250,000 medical, $500,000 evac/repat).
*   **Trip Cancellation:** Preferred has the highest limit ($15,000) versus Deluxe ($10,000) and Classic ($5,000).
*   **Baggage:** Preferred offers the highest limit ($8,000) versus Deluxe ($5,000) and Classic ($3,000).
*   **Travel Delay:** Preferred has the highest overall limit ($2,000) versus Deluxe ($1,500) and Classic ($1,000).
*   **Adventure Sports:** All tiers cover white water rafting under 'Adventurous activities', subject to conditions, with claims falling under relevant sections like medical or personal accident, where Preferred offers the highest limits.

Crucially, **none of these standard tiers cover pre-existing medical conditions like asthma**. Furthermore, trip cancellation due to a **rescheduled family wedding is highly unlikely to be a covered reason** under the standard terms of any tier; coverage is typically for unforeseen events like serious illness/death, natural disasters, etc.

Given the No Mix-and-Match rule and the fact that the critical requirements for pre-existing condition cover and the specific cancellation reason cannot be met by any of these standard tiers, the recommendation focuses on the tier providing the most comprehensive coverage for the *other* requirements. The Preferred tier offers substantially higher limits across all key areas (medical, cancellation for covered reasons, baggage, delay) compared to Classic and Deluxe. This provides greater protection for unexpected events (other than the excluded ones) during the trip to Australia, including the adventurous activity planned.

Therefore, the **Preferred** tier is recommended as it provides the highest level of protection for the requirements it *does* cover, despite the significant, unavoidable gaps regarding the customer's pre-existing condition and specific cancellation scenario within these standard plan options.
