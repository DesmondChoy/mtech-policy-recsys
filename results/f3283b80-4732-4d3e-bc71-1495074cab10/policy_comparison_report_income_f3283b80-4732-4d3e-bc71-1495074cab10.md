**Recommended Tier:** : Preferred

**Justification:**

Comparing the INCOME Classic, Deluxe, and Preferred tiers: All tiers offer core coverage like medical expenses, trip cancellation, lost luggage, travel delay, and scuba diving (under adventurous activities). However, a key customer requirement is "Accidental Death cover with double payout on public transport". The Classic tier explicitly does *not* offer this double cover benefit ("NA" in the policy details). Both the Deluxe and Preferred tiers include this feature.

Between Deluxe and Preferred, both meet the specific requirements listed (except for the pre-existing condition, which standard plans typically exclude). However, the Preferred tier offers significantly higher coverage limits across almost all categories compared to Deluxe:
*   **Medical Expenses Overseas (under 70):** $1,000,000 (Preferred) vs $500,000 (Deluxe)
*   **Trip Cancellation:** $15,000 (Preferred) vs $10,000 (Deluxe)
*   **Lost/Damaged Luggage:** $8,000 (Preferred) vs $5,000 (Deluxe)
*   **Accidental Death (under 70, base):** $500,000 (Preferred) vs $200,000 (Deluxe)
*   **Accidental Death (under 70, double cover):** $1,000,000 (Preferred) vs $400,000 (Deluxe)

Given the customer's request for "comprehensive" coverage and a "Flexible budget for good coverage", the substantially higher limits offered by the Preferred tier provide a more robust safety net, better aligning with the customer's stated priorities. While the pre-existing condition (Asthma) is not covered under this standard plan (a common exclusion), the Preferred tier provides the best overall fit among the available standard options based on the other requirements and the desire for strong coverage. The No Mix-and-Match rule means selecting the tier that best covers the *most* requirements with the *best* limits is necessary.
