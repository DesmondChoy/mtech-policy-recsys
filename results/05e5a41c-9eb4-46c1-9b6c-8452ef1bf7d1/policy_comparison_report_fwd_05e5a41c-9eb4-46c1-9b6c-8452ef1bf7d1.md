**Recommended Tier:** : First

**Justification:**

Comparing the FWD Premium, Business, and First tiers for the family's trip to Japan:

*   **Premium:** Offers the lowest coverage limits (e.g., S$200k Overseas Medical, S$7.5k Trip Cancellation). Crucially, the optional add-on for Pre-existing Medical Conditions only provides S$50k coverage overseas with a 50% co-payment for disruption/cancellation, which may not provide sufficient peace of mind for travel to Japan, especially involving skiing.
*   **Business:** Provides significantly better limits than Premium (e.g., S$500k Overseas Medical, S$10k Trip Cancellation). The Pre-existing Medical add-on offers S$100k overseas medical cover and S$10k cancellation (50% co-pay). This is a strong contender.
*   **First:** Offers the highest limits across all categories (e.g., S$1M Overseas Medical, S$15k Trip Cancellation). The Pre-existing Medical add-on provides S$150k overseas medical cover and S$15k cancellation (50% co-pay).

All tiers require the purchase of optional add-ons for Pre-existing Conditions, Pet Care, and potentially Sports Equipment Protector (for equipment loss/damage, as medical/accident cover for leisure skiing is typically included in base medical/accident benefits). The No Mix-and-Match rule means the family must choose one tier and purchase the necessary add-ons within that tier.

Given the customer's stated priority for "peace of mind", especially regarding the pre-existing condition (high blood pressure) and skiing activity in Japan (where medical costs can be high), the higher limits offered by the **First** tier, particularly the S$150k for pre-existing medical expenses via the add-on, align best with this requirement. While the Business tier offers good coverage, the First tier provides a greater buffer and more comprehensive protection, directly addressing the customer's main concerns, even though it is the most expensive option. Therefore, First is recommended over Business as it is *more* suitable, not just equally suitable, negating the need for the price tie-breaker rule.
