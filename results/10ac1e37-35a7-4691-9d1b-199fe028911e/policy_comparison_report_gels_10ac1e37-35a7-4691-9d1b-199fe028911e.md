**Justification:**

Comparing the GELS Basic, Gold, and Platinum tiers:
*   **Basic Tier:** Fails to meet key requirements. It explicitly excludes coverage for 'Adventurous leisure activities' (Section 37), meaning the customer's planned hiking would not be covered. Furthermore, it lacks 'Travel inconvenience for any reason' coverage (Section 19), making it highly unlikely that cancelling for a sister's wedding would be covered under its standard 'Trip cancellation' provisions (Section 16).
*   **Gold Tier:** Meets all core requirements. It includes coverage for 'Adventurous leisure activities' like hiking (Section 37). Crucially, it offers 'Travel inconvenience for any reason' (Section 19), which provides a potential mechanism (subject to conditions like purchase timing and typically a 50% co-payment on non-refundable costs) to cover cancellation for personal reasons like a sister's wedding, which standard cancellation clauses usually exclude. It also provides robust limits for medical ($500k overseas), luggage ($5k), and travel delay ($1.5k overseas).
*   **Platinum Tier:** Also meets all core requirements, including hiking and 'Travel inconvenience for any reason'. It offers higher limits than Gold across most categories (e.g., $1M medical, $8k luggage, $6k 'any reason' cancellation).

Given the No Mix-and-Match rule, the Basic tier is unsuitable. Both Gold and Platinum meet the functional requirements. However, the Gold tier provides the necessary coverage for hiking and the essential 'Travel inconvenience for any reason' benefit needed for the specific cancellation scenario, along with substantial core coverage limits. While Platinum offers higher limits, the Gold tier adequately addresses all stated needs and is ranked cheaper than Platinum according to the provided price ranking. Therefore, the Gold tier is recommended as the most suitable and cost-effective option that holistically meets the customer's requirements.
