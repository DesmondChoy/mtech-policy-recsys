**Recommended Tier:** : Gold

**Justification:**

Comparing the GELS GREAT TravelCare Basic, Gold, and Platinum tiers:
*   **Basic:** Offers the lowest limits for medical ($100k), evacuation ($200k), cancellation ($2k), and baggage ($2.5k). Crucially, it explicitly **does not cover** "Adventurous leisure activities" (Section 37), meaning the customer's required Hiking coverage is absent. It also lacks the "Travel inconvenience for any reason" benefit, making coverage for cancellation due to a wedding reschedule highly unlikely.
*   **Gold:** Provides significantly higher limits (Medical $500k, Evacuation $1M, Cancellation $10k, Baggage $5k). Importantly, it **explicitly covers** "Adventurous leisure activities" (Section 37), including Hiking. It also includes the "Travel inconvenience for any reason" benefit (Section 19), which offers potential (though likely partial, e.g., 50% payout, and conditional on purchase timing) coverage for the specific wedding cancellation scenario, unlike the Basic tier. It includes benefits like TCM, Dental, and Hospital Cash, absent in Basic.
*   **Platinum:** Offers the highest limits (Medical $1M, Cancellation $15k, Baggage $8k) and adds benefits like Sports Equipment cover (Section 33) and a shorter 4-hour trigger for travel/baggage delays. However, like Gold, it covers Hiking (Section 37) and includes the "Travel inconvenience for any reason" benefit (Section 19).

Given the No Mix-and-Match rule, the Basic tier is unsuitable due to the lack of Hiking coverage. Both Gold and Platinum meet the core requirements, including Hiking and offering a potential avenue for the wedding cancellation concern via the "Travel inconvenience for any reason" benefit. While Platinum offers higher limits and some extra benefits, the Gold tier already provides comprehensive coverage levels likely sufficient for the trip to Japan and meets all key requirements specified by the customer. Considering the customer's budget of "around $400", the Gold tier is more likely to align financially than the Platinum tier. Therefore, Gold represents the best balance of comprehensive coverage meeting all specified needs (especially Hiking) and likely budget alignment.
