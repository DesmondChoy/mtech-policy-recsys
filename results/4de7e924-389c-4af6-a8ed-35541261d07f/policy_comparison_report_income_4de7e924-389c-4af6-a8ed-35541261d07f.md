**Recommended Tier:** : Preferred

**Justification:**

Comparing the INCOME Classic, Deluxe, and Preferred tiers: All three tiers provide coverage for the customer's core requirements: medical emergencies, trip cancellation, lost or damaged luggage, and travel delays. The coverage limits for these core benefits increase significantly from Classic to Deluxe to Preferred (e.g., Medical Overseas: $250k/$500k/$1M; Trip Cancellation: $5k/$10k/$15k; Luggage Loss: $3k/$5k/$8k per person, respectively for adults under 70).

Crucially, none of the INCOME tiers explicitly list coverage for "additional pet hotel costs" due to travel delays as a specific, reimbursable benefit. However, all tiers include a "Travel Delay" benefit (Section 5) which provides a cash payout for delays exceeding six hours ($100 per adult for every six hours overseas delay in all tiers). While this cash benefit is not specifically earmarked for pet accommodation, it provides funds that the customer *could* use towards such unexpected costs. The Preferred tier offers the highest *overall limit* for this Travel Delay cash benefit ($2,000 per person compared to $1,000 for Classic and $1,500 for Deluxe), offering potentially more financial buffer for costs incurred during a prolonged delay.

Given the No Mix-and-Match rule (the customer must choose one tier), and the specific request for pet accommodation cover (which is not directly met by any tier), the Preferred tier is recommended. It provides the highest level of coverage for all core requirements (Medical, Cancellation, Luggage, Delay) and offers the highest overall Travel Delay cash benefit limit. This makes it the most suitable option to *indirectly* address the pet accommodation cost concern through the general delay allowance, while also providing the most robust protection for other potential issues during the trip to Japan. The customer's stated flexibility on budget supports choosing the tier with the most comprehensive coverage.
