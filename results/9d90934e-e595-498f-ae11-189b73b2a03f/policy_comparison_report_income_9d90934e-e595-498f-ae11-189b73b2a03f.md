**Justification:**

Comparing the INCOME Classic, Deluxe, and Preferred tiers: All three tiers provide baseline coverage for Trip Cancellation, Medical Expenses, Luggage issues, Travel Delays, and Hiking. However, they differ significantly in coverage limits. Classic offers the lowest limits (e.g., $5k Cancellation, $250k Medical). Deluxe increases these limits (e.g., $10k Cancellation, $500k Medical, Unlimited Evacuation). Preferred provides the highest limits (e.g., $15k Cancellation, $1M Medical, Unlimited Evacuation, $8k Luggage).

Crucially, based on the provided policy details for these standard tiers, **none** explicitly cover pre-existing conditions like high blood pressure ("Standard plans do not cover pre-existing medical conditions" noted in Classic/Deluxe, and assumed standard for Preferred unless otherwise stated). Furthermore, **none** of the provided tiers include coverage for Pet Accommodation Costs, a specific customer requirement. The coverage for additional accommodation/meals during travel delays is partially met; Trip Disruption covers accommodation up to a limit ($400/night, overall $3k in Preferred), but meals are not explicitly covered, and the Travel Delay benefit is a fixed cash sum per delay period ($100/6hrs in Preferred).

Given the customer's flexible budget, emphasis on feeling secure, and specific worries about comprehensive medical coverage and trip cancellation, the Preferred tier is recommended *despite its limitations regarding pre-existing conditions and pet care based on the standard plan data*. It offers the highest available limits for the covered risks (Medical, Cancellation, Luggage, Delay) among the three options, best aligning with the desire for maximum security for unforeseen events that *are* covered. The No Mix-and-Match rule means the customer must choose one tier, and Preferred provides the most robust protection overall for the covered items. The significant gaps regarding pre-existing conditions and pet accommodation must be noted as weaknesses.
